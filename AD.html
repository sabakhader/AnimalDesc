Pictures for Animals and there species

Mammals: These are warm-blooded vertebrates that typically have hair or fur and produce milk to feed their young. Examples include humans, dogs, cats, elephants, and whales.




Birds: Birds are warm-blooded vertebrates characterized by feathers, beaks, and laying hard-shelled eggs. Examples include sparrows, eagles, penguins, and ostriches.




Fish: Fish are cold-blooded vertebrates that live in water, have gills for breathing, and usually have scales. Examples include salmon, goldfish, sharks, and trout.




Reptiles: Reptiles are cold-blooded vertebrates with dry, scaly skin. They lay eggs on land and typically have clawed feet. Examples include snakes, lizards, turtles, and crocodiles.




Amphibians: Amphibians are cold-blooded vertebrates that typically go through metamorphosis, starting their lives in water (with gills) and transitioning to life on land (with lungs). Examples include frogs, toads, salamanders, and newts.
